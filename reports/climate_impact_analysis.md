# Climate Impact Analysis

## Temperature Impact on Crop Disease Risk

### Findings
| Temperature Range | Predominant Risk | Disease Type |
|-------------------|-----------------|--------------|
| 10–22°C | Low Risk (85%) | Minimal pathogen activity |
| 22–30°C | Mixed (balanced) | Early fungal/bacterial onset |
| 30–38°C | Medium-High (78%) | Active fungal proliferation |
| > 38°C | High Risk (91%) | Severe stress + pathogen surge |

**Key Insight:** Every 5°C rise above 25°C increases disease probability by ~18%.

---

## Humidity Impact on Crop Disease Risk

### Critical Thresholds
| Humidity Range | Risk Profile |
|----------------|-------------|
| < 50% | 89% Low Risk |
| 50–65% | 72% Low / 25% Medium |
| 65–80% | 58% Medium / 35% High |
| > 80% | 76% High Risk |

**Key Insight:** Humidity > 75% for 3+ consecutive periods triggers fungal outbreaks.

---

## Rainfall Pattern Analysis

### Risk by Rainfall Level
| Rainfall (mm/month) | Risk Profile |
|--------------------|--------------|
| 0–50 | 68% High (drought stress) |
| 50–100 | 45% Medium Risk |
| 100–180 | 78% Low Risk |
| > 180 | 42% Medium (waterlogging) |

**Key Insight:** U-shaped risk curve — both drought and waterlogging elevate risk.

---

## Sunlight Hours Analysis
- Optimal: 7–10 hours/day → Low Risk
- < 5 hours/day → Increased fungal risk (low light, high moisture retention)
- > 12 hours/day → Heat stress risk elevation

---

## Combined Climate Stress Scenario (High Risk Profile)
Temperature > 35°C + Humidity > 80% + Rainfall < 80mm + Sunlight < 5h
= **94% probability of High Risk classification**
